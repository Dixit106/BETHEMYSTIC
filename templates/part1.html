Brain Is Not Your Friend(Mostly):

You can probably think of times in your life when you were excited about something, like a holiday, party, hangout or a event --- but when it actually happened, it was just OK. Research has shown that this is common: we’re not always great at predicting what will make us most happy, and we don’t realise how bad we are at this.

The fact that we often judge the pleasure of an experience by its ending can cause us to make some curious choices.
--Prof Dan Gilbert, Stumbling on Happiness

If you missed your flight on the last day of an enjoyable holiday,you’ll probably remember the holiday as just OK.
This means we can’t just trust our intuitions;we need a more systematic way of working out which job is best for us.

Money makes you happier, but only a little:

I am not here to preach or debate about philosphy that can money buy happiness or not? But people commonly think more money is the main thing which will improve quality of their lives.
Is that true statistically?
Here are the findings from a huge survey in US in 2010:

This graphs show us if you are already being paid fair for your work no matter how much more money you start earning, your life satisfaction will not increase much.We can see here even when money is increased significantly life satisfaction haven’t increased much after 6.5 or 7.

Now if we look at day-to-day happiness, income seems even less important.”Positive affect” graph shows whether people reported feeling happy yesterday.

We can see that line goes almost flat around $50,000, showing that beyond this point, income had no relationship with day-to-day happiness in this survey.

The picture is similar if we look at the fraction who report being “not blue(emotionally stable) or “stress free” yesterday.

So all this data shows that lines are completely flat by $90,000.Beyond this point, income had no relationship with how happy, sad, or stressed people felt. This was suprizing for all so data was checked multiple time to see errors but it was same always.

High income improves evaluation of life but not emotional well-being,
--D. Kahneman and A. Deaton, 2010,

All this data doesn’t mean money isn’t important or you shouldn’t focus on it at all.It just means if you are already earning well, then more money will not give you more happiness and money should be one of the thing you focus on not the only thing you focus on.

Don't Aim for low stress:

Most prople want a job that’s not too stressful.It’s true and everyone know stress is bad for you but we don’t need stress we need eustress.So ya picture is a bit more complicated.

If we think about high-rank government and military leaders we might think that they will have high stress as they have more responsibility, more people to manage and less sleep mostly.But if we look at data it’s completely opposite they have less stress hormones and less anxiety.One explanation for this can be due to greater sense of control(setting schedules, dueties and thinking how to tackle challenges).
