You can probably think of times in your life when you were excited about something, like a holiday, party, hangout or a event --- but when it actually happened, it was just OK. Research has shown that this is common: we’re not always great at predicting what will make us most happy, and we don’t realise how bad we are at this.

The fact that we often judge the pleasure of an experience by its ending can cause us to make some curious choices.

This means we can’t just trust our intuitions;we need a more systematic way of working out which job is best for us.

Money makes you happier, but only a little:

I am not here to preach or debate about philosphy that can money buy happiness or not? But people commonly think more money is the main thing which will improve quality of their lives.
Is that true statistically?
Here are the findings from a huge survey in US in 2010:

This graphs show us if you are already being paid fair for your work no matter how much more money you start earning, your life satisfaction will not increase much.We can see here even when money is increased significantly life satisfaction haven’t increased much after 6.5 or 7.

Now if we look at day-to-day happiness, income seems even less important.”Positive affect” graph shows whether people reported feeling happy yesterday.

We can see that line goes almost flat around $50,000, showing that beyond this point, income had no relationship with day-to-day happiness in this survey.

The picture is similar if we look at the fraction who report being “not blue(emotionally stable) or “stress free” yesterday.

So all this data shows that lines are completely flat by $90,000.Beyond this point, income had no relationship with how happy, sad, or stressed people felt. This was suprizing for all so data was checked multiple time to see errors but it was same always.

High income improves evaluation of life but not emotional well-being,
--D. Kahneman and A. Deaton, 2010,

All this data doesn’t mean money isn’t important or you shouldn’t focus on it at all.It just means if you are already earning well, then more money will not give you more happiness and money should be one of the thing you focus on not the only thing you focus on.

6 Ingredients for Dream Job:

1.Work That's Engaging:

It's not salary, not status, not type of company and so on, what matters is what you do day by day and hour by hour.

Engaging work = work that draws you in, holds your attention and give sense of flow. Due to this reason hour spent editing a spreadsheet feels like forever and playing video game feels like time is running at 2x.As video games are made as engaging as possible.

Why are Video games engaging(corelate with work you would like to do [if you know])
1. Freedom to decide how to perform your work/task.
2. Clear tasks and goals.
3. Variety in the types of tasks.
4. Feedback, to know how well you're doing

2. Work That Helps Others

There are jobs which can be Engaging but they will lack meaning as they are not actually helpling other people that much. Although you should not underestimate a job thinking that this job is useless always research first.For example a video game streamer might be motivationg more people than an actual motivational speaker.

There's a lot of growing evidence that helping others is a key ingredient for life satisfaction. People who volunteer are less depressed and healthier.Global surveys also shows that people who donation to charity are as satisfied with their lives as those who earn twice as much.

3. Work You're Good At

Being good at your work gives you a sense of achivement, a key ingredient of life satisfaction.This will also give you power to ask for conditions like ability to work on meaningful projects, doing engaging tasks and earn fair pay.

This shows us SKILL>INTREST. If you are intrested in art but you are not good enough you will have to work on low level boring projects which you won't like at all.

All this does't mean you should only do work you are good at rather choose a meaningful intrest that you can develop as a skill and become good in it, become the very best top 1%.

4. Work With Supportive Colleagues

Obviously, if you hate your colleagues and work for a boss from hell, you’re not going to be satisfied at all. It is important to make friends at work which will mean there are people similar to you. The most important factor is whether you can get help from your colleagues when you run into a problem which means you need “social support” at work most of all.You also don’t need everyone to be your friend it’s not nessesary people who can disagree with you can provide you useful feedbacks.

A bad boss can ruin a dream position, while even boring work can be fun if done with a friend.

5. Work That Doesn't Have Major Negatives

Everything above is important but absence of things that would make work unpleasant is also imporatant as even 1 negative can outweigh many other positive factors.

1. A long commute
2. Very long hours
3. Pay you feel is unfair
4. Job insecurity

These might sound obvious, but people often overlook them.

6. Work That Fits With The Rest Of Your Life

You don’t need to find that one perfect job which get all ingredients of fulfilling life.You can have a high paying job which has less meaning but you can still help people with your money/charity.

Einstein had his most productive year in 1905, while working as a clerk at a patent office. While most of us would have thought to get a job as a scientist to work in labs somehow.

So this last factor is a reminder to consider how your career fits with the rest of your life.

Should U Just Follow Your Passion?

When we talk about career/future job most people now would just say “Follow your passion” do what you are intrested in. The main idea is that if you do what you like = happy life and career and we often see successful prople are passionate about what they do. So in some sense yes Follow Your Passion can work but Not Always.

Here are 3 ways “follow your passion” can be misleading advice:

1st Problem is that “follow your passion” suggests that passion is all you need. But we previously saw that if you lack 6 ingredients from above, you’ll be unsatisfied. If a basketball fan gets a job involving basketball, but works with people he don’t like, receives unfair pay, or find the work meaningless, they are still going to dislike their job.

In fact, “following your passion” can make it harder to satisfy the 6 ingredients, as the areas you’re passionate about are likely to be the most competitive, which makes it harder to find a good job.

2nd Problem is many people don’t feel like they have a career-relevant passion. If you don’t have a “passion,” don’t worry ---you can still find work you’ll become passionate about.

3rd Problem is that it can make people limit their options. What if there is a job you don’t know about or are ignoring because you don’t have passion in that field but when you finally did that job you enjoyed it more than your current job which you are passionate about.

It’s easy to have the idea that your “one true passion” will be immediately obvious, and eliminate options that aren’t immediately satisfying.

If you could find a nice job with 6 ingredients we talked about you could easily become passionate about that job even if you didn’t like it in the beginning.

Many successful people are passionate, but often their passion developes alongside their success and took a long time to discover, rather than coming first. Steve Jobs was passionate about Zen Buddhism. Then He got into tech, his passion grew with his success.

In reality, our interests change often, most of us are intrested in 100s of different things. Think back to what you were most interested in 5 years ago, and you’ll probably find that it’s different from what you’re interested in now. Another proof that we’re bad at knowing what really makes us happy.

ALL of this means you have more options for a fulfilling career than U think.

Do What Contributes

Our slogan for fulfilling career rather than “follow your passion would be: get good at something that helps others.
Or simply: do what contributes.

I prioritise helping other, having a meaningful work because if this would check the list you would be an asset to humanity, yourself, your family and everyone around you.

If you prioritise making a valuable contribution to the world first, you’ll develop passion for what you do ---you’ll become more content, ambitious, and motivated.



Here are 2 more reasons to focus on getting good at something that helps others.

1. You could be more successful

If you would do your best to help others naturally other good people would want you to be more successful too. You will also recive help when you need it. Also people with “giving mindset” end up among the most successful people too. This is both because they get more help, and they’re more motivated by a sense of purpose.


You need to be carefull though with everything you do in your life there should be balance. If you focus too much on others, and burn out you will end up unsuccessful so balance and 6 ingredients are required to some extent in our life.

Applying This To Your Own Career

These 6 ingredients, especially helping others and getting good at your job, can act as guiding light --- they’re what to aim for to find in a dream job long term.

Here are some exercises to help you start applying them:

1. Comparisons using 6 ingredients. Pick 2 options you are intrested in, then score them 1 to 5 on each factor.

2. 6 ingredients are the starting point there are other things that should not be ignored. We told past experiences are not very reliable but they still can’t be ignored.

These questions should give you hints about what you find most fulfilling:

• When have you been most fulfilled in the past? What did these times have in common?

• Imagine you just found out you’re going to die in 10 years. What would you spend your time doing?

• Can you make any of our six factors more specific? For example, what kinds of people do you most like to work with?

3. Now, combine our list with your own thoughts to determine 4 to 8 factors which are most important to you in a dream job.

4. Don’t expect to find an option that’s best on every dimension; rather, focus on finiding the option that’s best on balance.
