What is dream job for you? Some of you would say discovering passion and working in that field, while others would think key element of dream job is less stress and highly paid. But all these stuides and 3 decades of research into the cause of a satisfying life and career they didn't find much evidence for these views.

Then what did they find? They found 6 key ingredients of a dream job.Income and just “follow your passion” excluded suprizengly.In fact, just following passion blindely can lead you astra.Steve Jobs was passionate about Zen Buddhism before entering technology.Rather, you can develop passion by doing work that you find enjoyable and meaningful.The key is to get good at something that helps other people

To find a dream job, look for:
1. Work you're good at.
2. Work that helps others.
3.Supportive conditions: engaging work that makes you enter flow state, supportive colleagues, lack of major negatives like unfair pay, and work that fits your personal life.

Brain Is Not Your Friend(Mostly)

You can probably think of times in your life when you were excited about something, like a holiday, party, hangout or a event --- but when it actually happened, it was just OK. Research has shown that this is common: we’re not always great at predicting what will make us most happy, and we don’t realise how bad we are at this.

The fact that we often judge the pleasure of an experience by its ending can cause us to make some curious choices.
--Prof Dan Gilbert, Stumbling on Happiness

If you missed your flight on the last day of an enjoyable holiday,you’ll probably remember the holiday as just OK.
This means we can’t just trust our intuitions;we need a more systematic way of working out which job is best for us.
