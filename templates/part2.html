How Much Imapct Do Doctors Have?

Doctor’s Primary purpose is to improve health, so we tried to figure out how much extra “health” 1 doctor actually adds to humanity. We found, an average doctor in the UK over the course of their career will enable their patients to live about an extra combined 100 years of healthy life, either by extending lifespans or by improving overall health. Ofcourse, a huge amount of uncertainty is there in this figure, but real figure probably won’t be more than 10 times higer than this.

If we use a standard conversion rate (used by World Bank and other institutions). 30 extra years of healthy life = 1 life saved, 100 years of healthy life is equivalent to about 3 lives saved. This is surely a significant impact; however, it’s less of an impact than many people expect doctors to have over their entire career.

3 main reasons this impact is lower than you might expect:

1. Researchers largely agree that medicine = increased average life expectancy by a few years only. Most gains in life expectancy = due to better nutrition, improved sanitation, increased wealth, and other factors.

2. Doctors are only 1 part of the medical system, which also relies on a lot of other things, we all know.

3. There are already a lot of doctors in the developed world, so if you don’t become a doctor, someone else will be available to perform the most critical procedures. (Point to be noted this implyes for developed countries only)

This last point is illustrated by the chart below:

Here DALY = 1 year of life lost due to ill health per 100,000. We can see that the curve goes nearly flat once you have more than 150 doctors per 100,000 people. After this point (which all developed countries meet), additional doctors onl achive a small impact on average.

So if you become a doctor in a rich country like the US or UK, you may well do more good than you would in many other jobs, and if you are an exceptional doctor, then you’ll have a bigger impact than these averages. But it probably won’t be a huge impact.

Some Highest-Impact People In History

Despite this uninspiring statistic about how many lives a doctor saves, some doctors have had much more impact than this.

By 1968, it had been shown that a solution of glucose and salt, given via feeding tube or intravenous drip, could prevent death due to cholera. Still million of people were dying as this process was not that easy. Then while working in refugee camp on the border of Bangldesh and Burma, Dr. David Nalin sought to turn this insight into a therapy that could be used in poor rural areas. He showed that simply drinking a solution made at the right concentration and consumed at the right rate could be almost as effective as delivery via feeding tube or anything else.

Since then, this astonishingly simple treatment has been used all over the world, and the annual rate of child deaths from diarrhoea has plummeted from around 5 million to 1.5 million. Researchers estimate that the therapy has saved over 50 million lives to date, mostly children’s.

Surely if Dr Nalin had not been around, someone else would have discovered this eventually. However, even if we imagine that he sped up the roll-out of the treatment by only five months, his work alone would have saved about 500,000 lives. Another Medical research example would be Karl Landsteiner’s discovery of blood groups which has saved tens of millions of lives by enabling transfusions.

What Do These Differences In Impact Mean For Your Career?

We’ve seen some careers have had huge positive effects, and some have vastly more than others. We can’t really tell which careers will have the highest impact.

As somewhat this is due to luck – the people mentioned above were in the right place at the right time. You can’t guarantee you’ll make an important medical discovery.

So, what does this mean for you?

The key question should be: Don't ask, "How can I make a difference?" Almost any job makes some difference. Instead ask, "What is the best way to make the most difference?"

These examples above also show us, highest-impact careers aren’t always the obvious ones, we might think being a political leader would have more impact but we saw above how an officer in the Soviet military had more impact than any leader or scientist.

The Core Message:

Having a massive impact on the world requires strategic, analytical thinking, rather than just doing what feels traditionally helpful or following a passion. It is about maximizing your scale.

The Golden Rule: Don't Be a Villain

Trying to maximize your impact doesn't give you a free pass to do bad things. The "ends justify the means" approach is wrong. You still have to be a good person, respect human rights, and use common sense. So character still matters. You shouldn’t do something unethical just because it might lead to a good outcome later.
